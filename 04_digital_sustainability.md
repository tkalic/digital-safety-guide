# 04 — Digital Sustainability

## The Internet Has a Carbon Footprint

The internet feels weightless. It isn't.

Data centers consume roughly 1-2% of global electricity. A single Google search uses about 0.3 watt-hours of energy. Streaming one hour of video produces approximately 36 grams of CO2. Training a large AI model can produce as much carbon as five cars over their entire lifetimes.

None of this means you should stop using the internet. It means the digital world has real physical infrastructure with real environmental costs — and that choices, both individual and corporate, matter.

---

## Where the Impact Actually Comes From

**Data centers** are the biggest factor. They require constant power for servers and cooling systems. The question is where that power comes from. Some major providers (Google, Microsoft, Amazon) have made significant investments in renewable energy. Many smaller providers have not.

**Device manufacturing** is often overlooked. The carbon cost of producing a smartphone is significant — most of a device's lifetime environmental impact happens before you ever turn it on. This is why extending the lifespan of your devices matters more than most other individual actions.

**Network infrastructure** — the cables, routers, and towers that connect everything — also consumes substantial energy, though this is largely outside individual control.

---

## What Individuals Can Actually Do

Some advice in this space is genuinely useful. Some is performative and barely moves the needle. Here's an honest breakdown:

**High impact:**
- Extend the lifespan of your devices — don't replace what still works
- When you do replace devices, recycle them through certified e-waste programs (not general waste — electronics contain materials that are toxic in landfill)
- Choose cloud and hosting providers that use renewable energy where you have the choice

**Medium impact:**
- Lower streaming video quality when you don't need HD
- Unsubscribe from email lists you don't read — stored email has a small but real energy cost at scale
- Use energy-saving modes on devices

**Low impact (but not nothing):**
- Using Ecosia instead of Google (they plant trees with ad revenue)
- Deleting old files from cloud storage

---

## What Organizations Can Do

This is where the real leverage is.

- **Green hosting** — choose data center providers that run on renewable energy (Hetzner, Greengeeks, or check the Green Web Foundation's directory)
- **Efficient code** — bloated software requires more processing power, which requires more energy. Performance optimization is also sustainability optimization.
- **Hardware lifecycle policies** — repair and refurbish rather than replace on a fixed schedule
- **Remote work** — reducing commuting emissions is one of the most impactful things a company can do

---

## Honest Perspective

Individual actions matter less than systemic ones. The most impactful decisions are made by data center operators, device manufacturers, and policymakers — not by individual users choosing to delete old emails.

That said, collective individual behavior does shift markets over time, and being informed about these issues is a prerequisite for advocating for better systemic choices.
